AN ENVIRONMENT FOR EDUCATIONAL SERVICE COMMUNITIES
Josef Spillner
<p>In most global economies, there is a strong trend from agriculture and manufacturing towards service-orientation and&nbsp;tertiarisation: Services, products with value-added service solutions and, more recently, automated Internet service offerings&nbsp;seamlessly delivered through on-demand elastic cloud computing resources. In the affected societies, education is recognised&nbsp;as a key factor for maintaining the competitiveness. Specialised education about services is widely available, but tool support&nbsp;for hands-on learning and testing of how services can be produced, offered, delivered and improved is missing. We aim to fill&nbsp;this gap between theory and application by proposing an integrated environment for educational service communities such as&nbsp;service engineering classes. Initial results of our work show that the environment, which supports both auto-didactic learning&nbsp;and team-based competitive and collaborative learning-by-doing throughout the service lifecycle, motivates students and increases&nbsp;their practical knowledge about services. Our experience with the actual use of the environment in the context of a university&nbsp;course about web services and conclude the article with suggestions for future work are discussed.</p>
