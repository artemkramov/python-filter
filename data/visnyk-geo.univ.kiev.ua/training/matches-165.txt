PARADYNAMIC LANDSCAPE SYSTEM OF THE AZOV SEA REGION AS A FORM OF LANDSCAPE SPACE ORGANIZATION
Vorovka V.</p><p>Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv, Kyiv, Ukraine
/strong></p><p>One of the forms of landscape space organization, a paradynamic landscape system, is considered by the example of the Azov Sea coast. It is based on the idea of the existence of a specific type of landscape systems formed on the border of contrast environments. In this case – on the&nbsp;border of land and water (coastal zone), in other cases – on the border between mountains and plains, highlands and lowlands, forest and&nbsp;deforested landscapes, etc. The existence of such systems is grounded on the functional principle and strength of contrast-based interrelations.&nbsp;As opposed to the traditional landscape science, paradynamic systems are formed on the basis of interacting types of environments, distinct in&nbsp;their properties. In the study area, they are represented by the patches of land and sea bottom adjacent to the shoreline. The functioning of such a&nbsp;system depends not only on the interactions between natural but also on those between man-made landscapes of adjacent land and marine areas&nbsp;and their various modifications.<br>The publication describes the rationale for distinguishing the borders of the paradynamic landscape system. Such a border between land and&nbsp;sea bottom is represented by the line, to which extends the land impact on the sea and that of the marine environment on the coastal land. For the&nbsp;Ukrainian part of the Azov Sea coast these borders are distinguished as follows: on land it is a line of the watershed and climatic influence of the&nbsp;sea, in marine waters it is an isobath, to which extends the effect of waves on the treatment and redeposition of sediments.<br>The structure shaping links in the Azov Sea paradynamic landscape system are defined. The main land impacts on the sea are referred to the&nbsp;river runoff, coastal processes, and human activities. The sea effects on the land are classified as the climate impact, breeze circulation, and&nbsp;migration of living organisms. Specific examples of various links in the system are given.<br>An essential role in the functioning of these systems is played by human actions, which primarily manifests themselves in such coastal&nbsp;activities as fishing, marine transport, recreation and tourism, aquaculture. For the effective management of the shoreline, the borders of&nbsp;paradynamic coastal systems and their system-forming factors should be taken into consideration.<br>A detailed knowledge of the functioning of the Azov paradynamic landscape system is the basis for the integrated management of the coastal&nbsp;zone of the Sea of Azov. The latter, in addition to economic and social matters, requires a deep understanding of specific properties of the coastal&nbsp;environment. We cannot apply here a unified approach, since each coastal zone is characterized by its own unique conditions of the formation,&nbsp;development, and its own resource base thus requiring individual solutions and approaches to its integrated management. Basing on the long-term&nbsp;scientific data, this will enable the implementation of a comprehensive, well-planned and sustainable use of resources, functions and services of&nbsp;coastal zones. As a result, the maximum environmental and socio-economic efficiency of using the area can be reached. The knowledge of the&nbsp;intensity of coastal erosion and accumulation, characteristics of wind and hydrological regimes, characteristics of pollution and migration of&nbsp;contaminants etc. will assist in making adequate management decisions and preventing many unexpected problems in the future.</p><p><strong>Keywords:</strong>
landscape, coastal zone, paradynamic system, system borders, functioning, integrated management of coastal zones
