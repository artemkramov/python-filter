The function sequences and Taylor series with a fuzzy argument
I. Ya. Spektorsky
<p>The main consideration subject is functional sequences<em>f<sub>n</sub>(A)</em>with fuzzy number A for an argument. It is supposed that lim<sub>n→∞</sub><em>f<sub>n</sub>(x)=f(x)</em>and lim<sub>n→∞</sub><em>f<sub>n</sub>’(x)=f’(x)</em>, and these convergences are uniform on each interval within supp<em>A</em>. It is also supposed that the equation<em>f(x)=y</em>with respect to<em>x</em>has finite number of solutions for each<em>y</em>on each interval within supp<em>A</em>. The paper proposes sufficient conditions for<em>f<sub>n</sub>(A)</em>to converge in the sense that the sequence of membership functions<em>μ<sub>fn(A)</sub>(y)</em>: converges point-wise. It is proved that lim<sub>n<em>→∞</em></sub><em>μ<sub>fn(A)</sub>(y)= μ<sub>f(A)</sub>(y)</em>for all<em>y ϵ P</em>, except such<em>y=f(x)</em>, that<em>x</em>is a discontinuity point of<em>μ<sub>A</sub>(x)</em>, or<em>f‘(x)=0</em>. As a particular case of sequence<em>f<sub>n</sub>(A)</em>, the generalization of Taylor series<em>f(x)=∑<sub>i=0</sub><sup>∞</sup>(f<sup>(i)</sup>(x<sub>0</sub>)(x-x<sub>0</sub>)<sup>i</sup>/(i!))</em>is considered for real analytical function<em>f(x)</em>for the case of fuzzy argument<em>x=A</em>. Convergence of the series is considered in the sense of point-wise convergence of the partial sum<em>μ<sub>Sn(A)</sub>(y)</em>, where<em>S<sub>n</sub>(x)=∑<sub>i=0</sub><sup>n</sup>(f<sup>(i)</sup>(x<sub>0</sub>)(x-x<sub>0</sub>)<sup>i</sup>/(i!))</em>.</p>
