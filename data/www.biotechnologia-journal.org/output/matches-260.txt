/div><div>Coxsackievirus B3 (CVB-3) belongs to the Picornaviridae family of enterovirus genus of pathogens that cause<br><br>a great number of human diseases. A viral infection is associated with many pathological states such as:<i>myocarditis</i>,<br><br>dilated<i>cardiomyopathy, pericarditis, pleurodynia</i>, systemic infection in infants,<i>aseptic meningitis,</i>and<i>pancreatitis</i>.<br><br>Since viral diseases, especially in their chronic state, are difficult to treat, there has not been as yet, any<br><br>specific therapeutic developed against coxsackievirus till date. CVB-3 is a single stranded, positive-sense RNA<br><br>virus that encodes one large open reading frame flanked by two untranslated regions (UTR). The 5NUTR contains<br><br>an IRES element that directs the translation process and a cloverleaf structure that regulate viral replication. The<br><br>complementary, 3N terminal region of the replicative strand is also believed to be crucial for the replication of<br><br>events. The secondary structure RNA elements regulate the most important processes in the viral propagation<br><br>cycle. The mechanisms that rule the CBV-3 gene expression, its genome structure and the key steps of its viral<br><br>life cycle are being reviewed in the hope that better knowledge of these processes will lead to better understanding<br><br>of the molecular biology of CVB-3 and to the design of an effective therapy against this enterovirus.<
