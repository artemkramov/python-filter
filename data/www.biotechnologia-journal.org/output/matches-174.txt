/div><div>Germline mutations affecting the<i>BRCA1</i>and<i>BRCA2</i>genes explain 16-40% of breast and/or ovarian cancers aggregated<br><br>in families. Besides the<i>BRCA1/2</i>genes and several genetic factors associated with hereditary syndromes<br><br>which increase the risk of breast cancer, a considerable fraction of potential breast cancer predisposing<br><br>factors (-50%) remains unknown. It is presumed that candidate genes, functionally related to the<i>BRCA1/2</i>genes,<br><br>may account for some of the missing heritability. The<i>BARD1</i>gene, which encodes a protein indispensable for<br><br>BRCA1-mediated tumor suppression function and adequate apoptosis regulation, serves as a candidate breast<br><br>cancer susceptibility gene. Some initial reports indicated that<i>BARD1</i>is a plausible target for several pathogenic<br><br>mutations associated with increased breast and/or ovarian cancer risk. Nonetheless, further mutational studies<br><br>are necessary to determine the penetrance and role of the<i>BARD1</i>gene in cancer predisposition.<
