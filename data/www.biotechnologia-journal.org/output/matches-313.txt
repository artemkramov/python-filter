/div><div>Alternative splicing is an important part of mRNA processing which results in protein diversity in all eukaryotes.<br><br>In plants, this process is still poorly understood, however recent computational analysis has shown that alternative<br><br>splicing is far more prevalent than previously thought. For better characterization of alternative splicing in maize,<br><br>one of the most important crop species, we used AUGUSTUS (web-application deployed on local or remote host)<br><br>to predict multiple transcripts and alternative splicing events from maize chromosome 1 sequence. From over<br><br>300 million bp of chromosome 1, AUGUSTUS software predicted ab initio 46 400 genes and 20% of the estimated<br><br>genes have at least two transcripts. For 412 genes with three transcripts we performed additional analysis<br><br>including EST's alignment, protein identification and comparative evaluation with genes predicted using F genesh<br><br>and Gramene software. In consequence we have identified alternative splicing events for 42 genes from maize<br><br>chromosome 1.<
