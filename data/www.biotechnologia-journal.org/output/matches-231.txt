<i>Arabidopsis thaliana</i>inorganic pyrophosphatase (AtPPA1) coding DNA (ppa1 gene) was cloned into bacterial expression<br><br>vector and overproduced in<i>E. Coli</i>cells as a His-tagged protein. The recombinant protein was purified<br><br>from the bacterial lysate by two consecutive chromatographic steps: chelating chromatography on Ni<sup>2+</sup>-charged<br><br>resin followed by FPLC size exclusion chromatography. The homogenous protein was submitted for crystallization.<br><br>X-Ray diffraction data extending to 1.9Å resolution were collected using synchrotron radiation. The structure<br><br>was solved by molecular replacement and refinement is in progress (R-factor below 20%). The structure of<br><br>AtPP1 represents an alpha+beta protein fold which overlaps with other structural models for known bacterial and<br><br>yeast inorganic pyrophosphatases.</div
