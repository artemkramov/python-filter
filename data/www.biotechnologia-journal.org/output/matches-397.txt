/div><div>Chronic hepatitis C virus infection is a leading cause of progressive liver fibrosis, liver cirrhosis and hepatocellular<br><br>carcinoma. Iron overload is frequently observed in cases of chronic hepatitis C and has been suggested as a negative<br><br>prognostic factor for this disease. Although the mechanisms leading to iron accumulation are not fully explained<br><br>yet, both host and viral factors seem to contribute towards the development of this pathology. Better understanding<br><br>of the interplay between hepatitis C virus replication and expression of iron regulatory molecules may<br><br>elucidate new and interesting targets for the effective treatment of chronic hepatitis C.<
