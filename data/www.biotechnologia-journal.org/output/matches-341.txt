/div><div><i>Scutellaria barbata</i>wild type root cultures were grown on liquid media: Gamborg (B5) and Schenk-Hildebrandt<br><br>(SH) supplemented with (1 mg/l) indole-3-butyric acid (IBA). For each culture, growth rate and secondary metabolites<br><br>production were determined. It was observed that in the metabolic profiles, the dominating compounds were<br><br>flavone glycosides – wogonoside, baicalin and phenylethanoid glycoside acteoside. Aglycones namely wogonin,<br><br>baicalein and chrysin – were present at low concentrations. On B5 medium, the average productivity of plant material<br><br>counted as the sum of flavones was higher (ca. 1300 mg/l) than on SH medium (ca. 999 mg/l). In order to<br><br>remove the compounds that cause browning of the medium and inhibit culture growth, especially on B5 IBA 1.0<br><br>medium, a number of experiments with the addition of cork pieces, citric acid and Amberlite XAD-4 and XAD-7<br><br>resins into the culture media were performed. The best growth of the root culture was achieved on B5 medium<br><br>supplemented with – 1.0 mg/l IBA along with the addition of 7 mg/ml of cork and with 10 mg/ml of Amberlite<br><br>XAD-4 resin, although concentrations of the analyzed metabolites were lowers than in the control group.<
