/div><div>Genome shuffling is widely used for increasing the production of metabolites by bacterial strains, improving<br><br>substrate uptake as well as enhancing strain tolerance. This technique combines the advantage of multiparental<br><br>crossing allowed by DNA shuffling together with the recombination of entire genomes normally associated with<br><br>conventional breeding, or through protoplast fusion that increases the recombination process. The method of<br><br>genome shuffling was first presented by Stemmer and co-workers in 2002 when it was used to improve the<br><br>production of tylosin by Streptomyces fradiae. Nowadays, it is used in many experiments for increasing the<br><br>production of metabolites by bacterial strains, improving substrate uptake, and enhancing strain tolerance.<br><br>Genome shuffling is a major milestone in the strain-improvement technology and metabolic engineering.<
