/div><div>Zinc oxide nanostructures are interesting nanomaterials with a wide range of applications. Since the physical and<br><br>chemical properties of ZnO nanoparticles are influenced both by their shape and size, a control of morphology<br><br>of ZnO structures is needed for their commercial usage. Different chemical, physical, and biological methods used<br><br>to produce ZnO nanostructures can be found in the literature. The production of ZnO nanoparticles using so-called<br><br>green methods, using, for example, plant extracts or living organisms, is being investigated as these methods<br><br>are environmentally friendly and of low-cost. This review also discusses the trends in the biological synthesis of<br><br>semiconducting nanoparticles.<
