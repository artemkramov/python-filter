/div><div>Infections with human cytomegalovirus (HCMV) are widely spread worldwide. A clinical picture of the infection<br><br>varies and depends on the age of the patient and the condition of the immune system. Rapid dissemination<br><br>of HCMV is related to the presence of the virus in the peripheral blood cells. The most severe course of the disease<br><br>is observed in patients with congenital cytomegaly, organ transplant recipients and patients with either acquired<br><br>or inborn immune deficiencies. Chronic HCMV infection may lead to atherosclerosis and as a consequence,<br><br>to cardiovascular diseases such as: myocardial ischemia or infarction and hypertension. In addition, the relation<br><br>between HCMV infection and neoplastic transformation was noticed. Since diagnostic and therapeutic methods<br><br>are still imperfect, a development of an effective vaccine seems to be of key importance for a significant reduction<br><br>of the consequences of HCMV infection.<
