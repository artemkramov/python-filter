/div><div>The influence of different cultivation conditions (medium, pH values, temperatures of the process, air access) on<br><br>the ability of amino acid mineralization via oxidative deamination by<i>Serratia marcescens</i>(facultative anaerobic<br><br>bacterial strain) was examined. Among 11 tested substrates – amino acids serine and proline, of both optical configurations,<br><br>were chosen as model substrates for the determination of the acceptable range of the value of external<br><br>factors influencing the mineralization process. Both pH and oxygen access were found to be limiting factors.<br><br>While the acidic pH switched off the mineralization, restricted aeration significantly reduced the effectiveness<br><br>of the process, extending the time for its completion.<
