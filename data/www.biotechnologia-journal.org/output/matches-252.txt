/div><div>All guanine-rich DNA and RNA molecules tend to fold into inter- and intra-molecular structures called G-quadruplexes.<br><br>The core of a G-quadruplex is composed of at least two adjacent G-tetrads stabilized by monovalent cations.<br><br>While it has been suggested that there could potentially be over 375 000 quadruplex forming sequences<br><br>in the human genome alone, only a small number of three-dimensional G-quadruplex structures are known. More<br><br>structural data are needed to explain their presence and function<i>in vivo</i>. We offer here a short review of experimental<br><br>methods commonly used to determine G-quadruplex topologies.<
