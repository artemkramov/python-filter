/div><div>The study investigates the regeneration capacity of<i>Crambe abyssinica</i>via the callus phase. The conducted experiments<br><br>provide an overview of various conditions appropriate for callus development and its subsequent differentiation.<br><br>Explants from cotyledons and leaves of sterile 14-day-old seedlings of C. abyssinica cv. Mayer were used.<br><br>The primary callus so obtained was subsequently utilised for the observation of embryogenesis. The best callus<br><br>formation on the explants was achieved on the agar-solidified MS medium with 0.5 mg ·dm<sup>-3</sup>NAA and 0.5 mg ·dm<sup>-3</sup><br><br>BAP or TDZ. The formation of secondary embryogenic callus was most efficient on MS medium supplemented<br><br>with 0.4 mg ·dm<sup>-3</sup>NAA and 0.3 mg ·dm<sup>-3</sup>of BAP.<
