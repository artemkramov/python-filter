/div><div>This paper presents several analogs of nucleic acids, including their structure and selected physico-chemical properties,<br><br>reported recently as being obtained from non-nucleosidic units connected via phosphorodiester bonds.<br><br>These analogs are built from units that instead of sugar residues carry different diol structures functionalized<br><br>either with natural nucleobases or with other ring systems of an aromatic character. Some interesting nonnucleosidic<br><br>oligonucleotide analogs and their applications are described. Unlocked (UNA) and glycerol nucleic<br><br>acids (GNA) are also described.<
