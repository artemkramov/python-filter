/div><div>Proper sample storage before RNA isolation is crucial for a reliable assessment of microbial activity in activated<br><br>sludge. In our studies, different amounts of activated sludge expressed in mg of total suspended solids (TSS) were<br><br>fixed with a constant volume of RNA<i>later</i>RNA Stabilization Reagent (QIAGEN, Germantown, MD, USA) and<br><br>stored for 3 or 6 months. The results obtained point out that for the best RNA recovery, 1 mg of activated sludge<br><br>TSS should be fixed with 33 μl of the RNA<i>later</i>. It was also observed that a longer storage period (6 vs. 3 months)<br><br>of the activated sludge in RNA<i>later</i>lowered RNA recovery by, on average, 25 ± 15% although bacterial diversity<br><br>in all samples remained unaltered.<
