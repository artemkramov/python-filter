S-adenosyl-L-methionine (SAM) is the most common substrate used in biological methylation reactions. During<br><br>methyl group transfer, S-adenosyl-L-homocysteine (SAH) is formed as a byproduct. Since SAH is a strong inhibitor<br><br>of the ubiquitous SAM dependent methylation reactions, removal of SAH by enzymatic hydrolysis serves as an<br><br>important mechanism in the control of cellular methylation processes.</div
