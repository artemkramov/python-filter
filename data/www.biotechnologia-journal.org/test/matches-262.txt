/div><div><i><i>Rhizopus</i>oryzae</i>and<i><i>Rhizopus</i>microsporus</i>strains were screened for their ability to produce fumaric acid on glycerol<br><br>as the sole carbon source in the medium. After seven days of stationary culture, fumaric acid was assayed<br><br>by HPLC analysis, and maximum concentrations of 0.3% (w/v) and 0.33% (w/v) were recorded. Protoplast fusion<br><br>was used to improve fumaric acid production. A selective medium for the fusant culture was composed on the<br><br>basis of biochemical differences between parental strains, as examined using the Biolog FF MicroPlate<sup>TM</sup>Fungi<br><br>Identification Test. Double fusion rounds led to a 1.46-fold increase in fumaric acid productivity relative to the<br><br>parental strains. Individual<i>Rhizopus</i>fusants demonstrated a various ability to produce fumaric acid from<br><br>2.0% (w/v) of glycerol, with the most effective ones producing from 0.2 to 0.27 g · g<sup>-1</sup>this acid. To date, no studies<br><br>have been carried out to improve fumaric acid production by<i>Rhizopus</i>with the use of glycerol as the only<br><br>carbon source in the medium.<
