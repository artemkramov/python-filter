/div><div>This is a retrospective review on the anniversary of the first X-ray crystal structure of B-DNA dodecamer<br><br>CGCGAATTCGCG solved by R. Dickerson’s team at UCLA/CIT. Timely established conclusions, drawn from the<br><br>X-ray crystal structures of DNA oligomers, have explained some of the patterns of DNA oligomers interacting in<br><br>complex with DNA binding drugs and proteins. The studies provide us with the insight into sequence mechanics<br><br>and the sequence-dependent mode by which DNA double stranded helices interact with one another in a crystal.<br><br>How to extrapolate information from DNA X-ray crystal structures to physical characteristics and function of these<br><br>molecules in a solution remains a challenge. Nevertheless, these results are useful in the biophysical studies of<br><br>DNA molecules and may shed some light on how to approach nanotechnology from a fundamental science<br><br>perspective.<
