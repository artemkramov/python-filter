/div><div>Cry1Ab18 is an δ-endotoxin produced by<i>Bacillus thuringiensis</i>strain. Till date the detailed mechanism of this<br><br>toxin action is unclear. Therefore, solution of the three-dimensional structure of all Cry1 family members would<br><br>be desirable for a comprehensive understanding of the initial mechanisms that underlie the toxicity of this type<br><br>of toxin. Here, we predict a theoretical structural model of the newly reported Cry1Ab18 δ-endotoxin, using<br><br>a homology modeling technique with the structure of Cry1Aa toxin molecule (resolution 2.25Å). Cry1Ab18 resembles<br><br>Cry1Aa toxin by sharing a common three-domain structure. Domain I is composed of nine α helixes and<br><br>one small β strand, domain II is composed of nine β strands and two α helixes and domain III consists of two<br><br>α helixes and eleven β strands. This model supports the existing hypotheses of receptor insertion and will further<br><br>provide the initiation point for the domain swapping experiments aimed towards improving protein toxicity, and<br><br>will help in the deeper understanding of the mechanism of action of common toxins.<
