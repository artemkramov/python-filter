/div><div>Experiments were carried out to develop micropropagation protocols for cultivars of hybrid origin which have<br><br>been not cultivated via tissue culture. Proliferating cultures of<i>Pelargonium × domesticum</i>“Tip Top Duet” and<br><br>“Black Knight” were obtained even if initially the number of excised aseptic nodal pieces was considerably reduced<br><br>due to contamination with endogenic bacteria. The supplementation of a maintenance medium with either<br><br>100 or 200 mg/l peptone proved beneficial. The best propagation coefficient, exceeding 82 adventitious shoots<br><br>from one initial microcutting, was obtained on a modified MS medium with an addition of 200 mg/l peptone and<br><br>0.5 mg/l adenine. The regenerated shoots readily developed new, anatomically properly formed leaves. In the<br><br>plant material studied, the leaf epidermis produced glandular trichomata which were similar in structure to those<br><br>observed in a standard cultivar.<
