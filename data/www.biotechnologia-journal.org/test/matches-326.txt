/div><div>The most general definition of transcriptome assumes that it is a complete set of RNA molecules (transcripts)<br><br>that are formed in a given organism. In response to exogenous and endogenous stimuli, transcriptome undergoes<br><br>constant qualitative and quantitative changes. Monitoring these changes can provide information on how the living<br><br>organisms function and deal with the changing environmental conditions. Recently, a new approach, called<br><br>RNA sequencing or RNA-Seq has been applied to transcriptome-oriented studies. It is based on the next-generation<br><br>sequencing and allows transcript discovery and quantification. This new approach permits identification of<br><br>novel transcripts and products of their processing or partial degradation. Since RNA-Seq does not require information<br><br>about the sequence of the reference genome, it can be used for transcriptome analysis in any organism.<br><br>This feature combined with the high sensitivity and broad dynamic range of RNA-Seq experiments, make them<br><br>much more versatile for high-throughput studies as compared to DNA microarray technology.<
