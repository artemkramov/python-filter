/div><div>Protein identification is usually achieved by tandem mass spectrometry (MS/MS). Because of the difficulty in<br><br>measuring complete proteins using MS/MS, typically a protein is enzymatically digested into peptides and the<br><br>MS/MS spectrum of each peptide is measured. The database searching methods are predominant in the task of<br><br>peptide identification. Their aim is to find the best match between model spectra generated from the peptides<br><br>stored in the database and the experimental mass spectrum obtained for an unidentified peptide. In this approach<br><br>one assumes that the peptide under investigation belongs to the scanned database. Otherwise, so called de novo<br><br>methods have to be applied to determine the peptide sequence. Unfortunately, de novo sequencing algorithms<br><br>are fragile in the presence of missing peaks, background noise or post-translational protein modifications. In this<br><br>paper, we propose a post-processing method for optimizing the results obtained from de novo sequencing<br><br>algorithms. Our approach in the reconstruction of amino acid sequences employs only spectral features and is<br><br>robust with respect to missing data. We demonstrate the significant improvement achieved using our method<br><br>applied to sequences reconstructed using a popular de novo sequencing method. The tool is freely available at<br><br>http://pepygen. sourceforge.net.<
