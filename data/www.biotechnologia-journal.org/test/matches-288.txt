/div><div>The suitability of<i>in vitro</i>culture technique for vegetative propagation of<i>Biscutella laevigata</i>calamine ecotype<br><br>was investigated. Various organic additives were tested as medium supplements: squash obtained from fleshy<br><br>pineapple fructification, liquid endosperm of coconut, and a conditioned medium acquired from the culture of<br><br>green algae. Exploited plant-derived biostimulators along with growth regulators included in the propagation<br><br>medium, improved<i>B. Laevigata</i>multiplication in vitro. The highest micropropagation coefficient, exceeding three<br><br>adventitious rosettes from one maternal microcutting, was obtained in the medium containing 10 ml A l<sup>-1</sup>pineapple<br><br>pulp during a 6-week-long culture. Microrosettes were rooted in vitro in a hormone-free, modified propagation<br><br>medium, with macro- and microelements accounting for one third of the initial concentration. Acclimatization<br><br>to ex vitro environment was conducted both in growth chamber and greenhouse conditions. Plants cultivated<br><br>in the greenhouse bloomed and set seed. The results of the experiment allowed determination of the most<br><br>suitable conditions required for culture initiation and successful development of a protocol for in vitro propagation<br><br>of<i>Biscutella laevigata</i>ecotype obtained from the Olkusz district.<
