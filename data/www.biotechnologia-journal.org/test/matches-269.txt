/div><div>The biotechnological application of genetically modified organisms (GMOs) in agriculture has increased to help<br><br>improve the production of vegetable protein and to prevent a myriad of possible viruses resulting from genetic<br><br>mutation. Among other products of GMO biotechnology are materials for medical treatment and pharmaceuticals.<br><br>The movement has increased concern and discussion about the risk and profitability of reprogramming genetic<br><br>pathways for the cultivation of plants and vegetables. This paper provides a brief overview of different perspectives<br><br>on the topic relating it to aspects of pioneering agro-biotechnology and human function viewed from the<br><br>standpoint of an outsider to the world of GMO agriculture.<
