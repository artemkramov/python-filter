/div><div>MicroRNAs (miRNAs) are a class of non-coding RNAs with important role in gene regulation in various organisms.<br><br>These RNAs regulate gene expression at the post-transcriptional level. To date, several hundred plant miRNAs<br><br>have been deposited in the miRBase database. Many of them are conserved during the evolution of terrestrial<br><br>plants, suggesting that the well-conserved miRNAs may also retain homologous target interactions. So far, there<br><br>has been no experimental or computational identification of miRNAs and their target genes in<i>Thellungiella halophila</i>.<br><br>Here, using a computational homology based search approach and according to a series of filtering criteria,<br><br>a total of 8 miRNAs belonging to 4 miRNA families were detected from the Expressed Sequence Tags (EST) databases.<br><br>Potential target genes of these predicted miRNAs were subsequently assessed. Our findings showed that<br><br>among the target genes, most of them encode transcription factors and enzymes that participate in regulation<br><br>of development, growth and other physiological processes.<
