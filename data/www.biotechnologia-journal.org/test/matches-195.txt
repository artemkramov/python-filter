/div><div>Actin-1 is a ubiquitous protein belonging to the reproductive class of Actin family in<i>Arabidopsis thaliana</i>. This<br><br>protein is involved in the formation of filaments that are major components of the cytoskeleton. Despite the importance<br><br>of this protein, very little information is available regarding its structure and function in plants. In this<br><br>study, analysis of the protein sequence was done and comparative model of Actin-1 was constructed (UNIPROT<br><br>ID: P0CJ46) from<i>Arabidopsis thaliana</i>using the crystal structure of Dictyostelium discoideum actin (PDB ID:<br><br>1NLV-A) as template employing Modeller version 9.9. The stable structure was generated by 5 nanosecond molecular<br><br>dynamics simulation steps using GROMOS43A1 96 force field that characterized its structural and dynamic<br><br>feature. The biochemical function of the final simulated structure was also investigated using PROFUNC. The<br><br>molecular simulation study suggested that the modeled Actin-1 protein retain its stable conformation in aqueous<br><br>solution. The predicted binding sites in the modeled Actin-1 protein are very informative for further protein-ligand<br><br>interaction study.<
