The sequencing of a protein is a complicated issue. Several methods have been developed in order to establish<br><br>the amino acid sequences of proteins, including Edman degradation, LC/MS/MS and cDNA sequencing. A number<br><br>of examples confirm that X-ray crystallography could also be a useful tool for the identification of an amino acid<br><br>sequence of unknown proteins. Here, we present a short review summarizing the application of crystallography<br><br>for protein sequence determination and identification.</div
