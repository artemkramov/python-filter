The UvrA protein is a DNA-binding and damage-recognition enzyme which participates in the prokaryotic type<br><br>nucleotide excision repair (NER) pathway. It has recently been noted that some bacterial genomes comprise<br><br>additional uvrA genes which encode five distinct types of UvrA homologue. We investigated the sequences of over<br><br>2400 bacterial genomes and found 130 examples of bacteria containing<i>uvrA2</i>genes. The sequence analyses<br><br>conducted on these UvrA homologues revealed that the previously established division of UvrA proteins might<br><br>be based on some incorrect assumptions. In this paper, we present the reasons for our creation of a new division<br><br>of UvrA homologues and a description of the four UvrA classes we have created.</div
