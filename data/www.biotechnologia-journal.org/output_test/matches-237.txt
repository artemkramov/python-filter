Nodules are unique root organs of legume plants. Nodulation (nodule formation) is an extraordinary symbiotic<br><br>process which eventually allows a plant to assimilate atmospheric nitrogen. The nitrogen-fixing bacteria that colonize<br><br>the nodules convert molecular nitrogen N2 into compounds suitable for plant metabolism. A class of plant<br><br>hormones called cytokinins are known to affect the nodulation process. A small protein, Nodulin 13 from<i>Medicago<br><br>truncatula</i>(MtN13), is expressed in the outer cortex of the nodule at early stages of nodulation. MtN13 belongs<br><br>to class 10 of plant pathogenesis-related proteins (PR-10). PR-10 members have been shown to bind cytokinins.<br><br>This paper focuses on the involvement of cytokinins in nodulation and presents a brief overview of the<br><br>crystal structure of MtN13 in complex with trans-zeatin, a naturally occurring cytokinin.</div
