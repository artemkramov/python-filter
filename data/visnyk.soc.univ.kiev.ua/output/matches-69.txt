Participation and Political Regimes in Contemporary Europe: Comparing non-EU 'New Eastern Europe' with 'Old' and 'Recent' EU Member States.
Yu. Savelyev
<p>The paper attempts to provide a systematic view on different types of participation (political, civic and social) and compares trends<br>and patterns of participation between clusters of European societies. Such clusters comprise, first, non-EU 'New Eastern Europe',<br>second, other Central and Eastern European societies, which accessed the EU recently, third, Western European countries, which have<br>been advanced and stable democracies for decades, fourth, Mediterranean EU members. We differentiate various contexts of&nbsp;participation including political opportunity structure and political regimes that is crucial for adequate comparison of participation&nbsp;patterns. The theories and measurement models, most of which were elaborated to explain patterns and factors of participation based&nbsp;on data from Western democracies, are critically examined in the context of 'New Eastern Europe' societies. Existing political&nbsp;opportunity structures as two dimensional political regimes are identified to show similarities and differences in patterns in participation&nbsp;in countries with various socio-economic conditions. Available indicators of level of democracy, political rights and civil liberties are&nbsp;employed to assess political regimes in European societies. The paper suggests research questions and hypotheses to explain&nbsp;peculiarities of participation in 'New Eastern Europe' countries.</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Keywords</h4><br><div>Civic participation; political participation; social participation; political regimes; political opportunity structure; 'New Eastern Europe'.
