MOTHERING AS CARE PRACTICES: CONCEPTUAL ASPECTS OF ANALYSIS
O. Strelnyk
The aim of the paper is to review the conceptions that enable to avoid care psychologization and go beyond the relationship of mother and child in its interpretation. One of such conceptual schemes is A. Giddens's structuration theory since practice concept makes it possible to pass from the universalization and essencialisation of motherhood to the analysis of various care practices and their time and space dimensions. It is noted that modern mothers' practice is a constant balancing act between private and public, between family and work, between parental and professional responsibilities.</div><br></div><div><h4>Keywords</h4><br><div>motherhood/mothering; practice; care; structuration theory; resources of practices; time; temporality.
