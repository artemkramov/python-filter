The concept of knowledge credibility and problem of uncertainty in empirical sociological study
М. Kravchuk
<p>The article considers the problem of the credibility of knowledge in empirical sociological research. The author makes a refinement of the&nbsp;concept of credibility, analyzing it as an indicator of true knowledge. Building on the achievements of the researchers, as E. Borel, P.Kopnin,&nbsp;V.Volovich determines the ratio of the probability and reliability characteristics as evidence of knowledge. Particular attention is paid to the relation&nbsp;of concepts of uncertainty and certainty of knowledge. Also performed clarifying the content and scope of the notion of error and uncertainty. On&nbsp;the basis of theoretical achievements A. Seidel, Y.Kemnits, L. Brillouin analyzes the causes of errors and perform their classification. The author&nbsp;analyzes the concept of elementary and complex error indicates the additive nature of the error. The publication notes that the notion of error refers&nbsp;to the incompleteness of knowledge related to the data. At the same time uncertainty covers the entire scope of the incompleteness of new&nbsp;knowledge as a result of the study as a whole and can not be reduced to the sum of complex errors. The concept of uncertainty is considered as&nbsp;indicators relevant to the probability characteristics as evidence of knowledge. Methodological background of the research is the concept of&nbsp;postnonclassical science of V. Stepin</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Keywords</h4><br><div>credibility; probability; error; uncertainty
