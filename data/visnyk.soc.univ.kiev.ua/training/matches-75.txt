Gender inequality in the labor market of Ukraine: Structural preconditions and lasting stereotypes
O. Shestakovskyi
<p>It is described structural preconditions and the current state of gender inequality in the Ukrainian labor market. The data on virtual horizontal<br>and vertical segregation of women and their higher load of household chores are adduced. It is shown that besides objective reasons, gender&nbsp;inequality is reproduced by stereotypes which prevalence among individuals of both genders have not almost changed in time. Among the&nbsp;steroyypes are beliefs about desirability of letting men have workplace, lesser suitability for executive positions and, especially contradictory role&nbsp;expectations that maintain women's "double burden".</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Keywords</h4><br><div>gender inequality; labor market; double burden; gender stereotypes
