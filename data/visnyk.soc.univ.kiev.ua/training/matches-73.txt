Life-course Strategies and Labor Migration: Ukrainian in Italy and Poland
O. Oleinikova
<p>Current labor migration from post-Soviet Ukraine to the European Union' countries is characterized by significant scale and&nbsp;intensity, innovative character, diversity and unexpectedness of life collisions. Among all the EU countries, Italy and Poland emerge&nbsp;as a new migration space for labor migrants from post-Soviet Ukraine. The purpose of this paper is to examine post-Soviet Ukrainian&nbsp;labor emigrants to Italy and Poland and to reveal their strategies of adaptation their individual life paths to the new environment. It is&nbsp;identified and discussed the relationship between life course strategies and their realization via international labor migration during&nbsp;the last decade of the 20th century. The research paper proposes highly relevant life strategy models of Ukrainians based on the&nbsp;study of intentions, means and results of life strategy implementation as well as of values, target orientations and the rationality of&nbsp;life strategy choice by migrants. The study is based on the qualitative research conducted during 2009-2011, using the method of&nbsp;discourse analysis of transcripts and "thick descriptions" of in-depth interviews with Ukrainian labor migrants in Italy and Poland.&nbsp;The following strategy models were analyzed: a strategy of achievement and a survival strategy. The empirically based analysis gave&nbsp;evidence concerning an ambivalent life strategy models widespread among the Ukrainian labor migrants, which can be defined as&nbsp;either a strategy of achievement or survival strategy.</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Keywords</h4><br><div>labor migration; life-course perspective; strategy of achievement; survival life strategy; post-Soviet social transformations
