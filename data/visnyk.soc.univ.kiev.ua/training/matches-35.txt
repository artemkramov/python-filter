Path to gender equality in education (Experience of gender audit at Faculty of Sociology of Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv)
S. Babenko, L. Males, O. Kharchenko, T. Chervinska
<p>The article is focused on the results and the outcomes of the gender audit conducted at the Faculty of Sociology Taras Shevchenko National&nbsp;University of Kyiv in September-October 2015 within the frame of the project "Gender Mainstreaming in Universities of Ukraine". This project&nbsp;involved a participative gender audit in 15 Universities of Ukraine on the basis of the methodology and procedures developed by the International&nbsp;Labor Organization (ILO) in order to assess the achievements of organizations in terms of gender equality. The study assessed the relevance of&nbsp;gender equality for education and sociological education in particular, gender stereotypes spread in professional and private spheres, especially&nbsp;the gender bias in education and the labor market. Also some recommendations to improve the situation in terms of the proceeding gender equality&nbsp;are formulated. Gender audit is proposed as a research strategy to provide a feedback to the reforms in Universities and higher education. In&nbsp;particular reforms of educational organizations, social relations among students, professorship, administration at universities, relevance change of&nbsp;gender bias in education and labor market on the way to gender equality.</p></div><br></div><div><h4>Keywords</h4><br><div>gender equality; gender audit; gender bias; gender stereotype; gender sensitivity
