<span>Abstract</span></strong><br><span>Pluto’s planetary system, because of its remoteness, the recent discovery of components and lack of observational data remains understudied. Modern approach to re-processing early observations of Pluto with new technologies and measurements can be an effective treatment for building models of planetary systems and the theory of motion. As a result of combining the efforts of three Ukrainian observatories – the project participants UkrVO – collected, digitized and processed observations of Pluto during 1961–1990 included in the Joint Digital Archive of UkrVO. Using common techniques astronomy negative digitization and further processing for observations obtained 5 telescopes catalogue positions and magnitudes of Pluto. An analysis of the (O-C) in the position and magnitude relative to the current standard ephemeris motion of the planet and comparison with the results of other authors treatments.</span></p><p><span><strong>Keywords
